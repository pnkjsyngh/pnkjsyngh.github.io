Pankaj Singh

I am a Modeling and Simulation Engineer at Corning Inc., where I work on developing computational tools for glass products R&D and production. At Corning I am working on chemical strengthening using Ion Exchange and relaxation mechanisms of glass.

In the past, I have co-founded a bio-motion analytics company Iterate Labs to reduce workplace injuries and improve workforce productivity.

I did my PhD at Cornell Univeristy in the Field of Theoretical and Applied Mechanics under the joint guidance of Herbert Hui and Anand Jagota. For my doctoral thesis I worked on multiscale modeling of synaptic vesicle docking and fusion. I did my Bachelors and Masters in Mechanical Engineering at Indian Institute of Technology, Kanpur, India and for my Masters thesis I worked on computational modeling of curvature sorting of proteins on cell membranes.

Research

I'm interested in developing mathematical models and computational techniques for physical systems. So far, I have applied these tools towards problems in solid mechanics, biophysics, ergonomics and glass research.

Currently, I am reading and following literature in the fast evolving field of Physics Informed Neural Networks (PINNs).
